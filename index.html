Field notes

A small machine for daily devotion.

  1. 01

    One verse, every minute, all day.

    FaithClock holds 1,599 verses from the Berean Standard Bible. Each minute of the twelve-hour clock points to a chapter and verse — at 3:16, John 3:16. At 11:28, Matthew 11:28. There is no minute without Scripture. When several passages match a single moment, the clock rotates them, day by day, so you keep finding new ones.

    Berean Standard Bible · Public Domain
  2. 02

    Your photos, set as the page.

    Pick any album from your iPhone or iPad — family pictures, the mountains, kids on the swings. They become the canvas the verse rests on. Six gentle transitions between them; rotate as slow as every ten minutes or as quick as every one. Nothing leaves your device.

    Photos via PhotoKit · Local-only
  3. 03

    Designed to be looked at.

    Six serifed fonts for the verse. Seven for the clock — including a real seven-segment display lifted straight from a 1970s digital readout. Four sizes. It looks at home on a kitchen counter or a bedside table; on a glass-front iPad mini in the morning or a 13-inch iPad Pro in the afternoon.

    Typography · Cross-iPad layout
  4. 04

    Yours alone. No exceptions.

    No accounts. No analytics. No internet required. No advertising, ever. No in-app purchases, ever. One purchase — $1.99 — and the only data that leaves your device is the dollar you spend to download it.

    Privacy · Offline-first
